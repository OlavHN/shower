Websites vs WebApps

As said many times already. It's all web technologies in the end. Yet, from an end users point of view using webapps is a complete new experience on the web.

The user experience of WebApps

Instead of googling or remembering urls, the user can discover the website, or webapp, in a marketplace. The user then installs the website. Even seamlessly paying for it if it costs money.

The user experience of WebApps

The website can then be launched when needed from the homescreen.

The user experience of webapps

The website can even delegate and subscribe to tasks going on on the device. Just like android intents. In fact, the entire experience for someone who doesn't know anything about the underlying technology is just like the experience on traditional smartphones. People seem to prefer interacting with apps over websites on mobile, so this gives the possibility for websites to provide the exact same user experience.

Gracefully handle connectivity

Of course, even tho it's possible to give the user experience of an app doesn't mean it happens just because you install the website, rather than visiting it. One such major hurdle is that unlike app developers, web developers assume the user has internet connectivity. Apps running on Firefox OS should really make an effort to handle connectivity gracefully.

Design guidelines

Mozilla has come up with a few guidelines that they follow in their core apps.

Left and right header navigation

Like the iPhone, the Firefox phone only has one button - a home button. Back navigation, cancel button and other "negative" buttons belong in the top left corner. "Positive" buttons that confirms, saves or take you further into the app belongs in the top right corner. App specific actions then have room on the lower toolbar.

Left and right layered drawers

Many of the applications have layered content revealed though drawers. Similar to the buttons, the convention here is that drawers that doesn't move you further into the app, like the calendar settings here, are on the left side, while drawers that take you further into the app belong on the right side. In this example the browser tabs are accessed though drawer on the right side.

Productivity apps vs media apps

The core apps that ships with the device are split into two categories. Productivity and Media. Productivity apps are effective, bright and easy to use. The iPhone was my first smartphone and it used a lot of skeuomorphs, or design analogies to quickly teach the user how to use an iPhone. The Firefox Phone will be the first smartphone for a lot of it's users and the productivity apps relies on a skeuomorphic design to quickly get it's users up to speed. The design of the media apps is a little more brave. They follow a dark color scheme, removes chrome and push the media content to the front.

Getting started

The core apps follow these guidelines and visual expression, but apps in general are free to chose which, if any of them, to follow. If you choose to let your app blend in with the experience the core apps provide here are a few pointers on how to get started.

Header example

        @import url(resources/headers.css);
      
        <section role="region">
          <header>
            <menu type="toolbar">
              <a href="#"><span class="icon icon-edit">edit</span></a>
              <a href="#"><span class="icon icon-add">add</span></a>
            </menu>
            <h1>Messages</h1>
          </header>
        </section>
This snippet is copied directly from the building blocks. The building blocks are the design elements that the core apps are build with and they are located in a restricted area of the phone. The recommended way of using these building blocks is including a copy of them in your app.

Header example

The snippet will produce the topmost header here, with all interaction states. The other headers are also made equally simple.

Tons of pre made building blocks

Since all the core apps are build with these building blocks there are a lot of them. All equally simple to reuse in apps.
